In 1989, after my first semester at university, I made the first line on the schematics for a small self-designed and self-built computer. This computer, based on the 6502 CPU, has until 1992 become a small computer system. But from then I only occasionally worked on it.

Well, after someone asked me to release it to the public, I decided to overhaul the documentation, translate it to english and put it under the GNU public license.

Version 1.3.10 now supports the C64, including the new fast serial RS232 interface by Daniel Dallmann, and has some better NMI support. Here is a short introduction to what has changed since the last published version, 1.3.6g.

Well, when I did this project, it was just for fun. But now I find it quite nice. Well, if you know some magazin that would like to publish some of it, I will be glad writing an article or so (if anybody really wants it ;-)
But on the other hand my interests have moved. I will gladly receive patches and fixes and even new programs - but I will not continue working on it (at least for a considerable time). I have other things to do (although this stuff is real fun!)
